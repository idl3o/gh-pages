The Laplacian Operator

The Laplacian operator is a differential operator denoted by the symbol ∇², and defined as the divergence of the gradient of a function. It is used in many areas of mathematics, physics, and cryptography.

\[\nabla^2 f = \nabla \cdot \nabla f\]

In cryptography, Laplacian-based functions can be used for:

  • Hash functions with strong diffusion properties
  • Elliptic curve algorithms
  • Random number generation
  • Secure multi-party computation
