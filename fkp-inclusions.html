Fermat's Theorems

\[x^n + y^n = z^n\]

Fermat's theorems explore integer solutions to polynomial equations, most famously Fermat's Last Theorem which states that no three positive integers x, y, and z satisfy the equation for any integer value of n greater than 2.

Pauli Exclusion Principle

\[|\psi\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(|↑↓\rangle - |↓↑\rangle)\]

The Pauli Exclusion Principle states that no two identical fermions can occupy the same quantum state simultaneously. This fundamental principle underlies atomic structure and chemistry.
